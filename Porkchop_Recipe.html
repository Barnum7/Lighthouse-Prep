Tender Porkchops

Porkchops are a delicious staple of a budget diet. However, they're very easy to overcook. When this happens you're left chewing something completely indistinguishale from rubber.

Today we're going to be learning how to make pork chops fit for a king. On a pauper's budget.

Okay, now that I have you completely, 275% sold on the idea of making porkchops RIGHT NOW let's cover what supplies we'll need.

Ingredients

Here's what you're going to need for the brine (optional) and for the porkchop itself

For the brine:

  • 3 cups of cold water, divided
  • 3 tablespoons coarse kosher salt (or 2.5 tablespoons table salt)
  • Optional flavorings: 2 smashed garlic cloves, 1/2 teaspoon black peppercorns, 1 bay leaf

For the pork chops:

  • 2 to 4 pork chops - center cut, bone on, 3/4 inch to 1-inch thick (about 1 pound each)
  • Olive Oil
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Equipment

  • Shallow dish (for brining)
  • Large cast iron, stainless steel, or other oven-safe skillet
  • Tongs

Instructions

For the Brine (optional)

  1. Place 2 cups of water in the shallow dish, and bring 1 cup of water to a bowl
  2. Place 3 tablespoons of coarse kosher salt (or 2.5 talespoons of table salt) into the shallow dish
  3. Place the garlic clove, peppercorns, or bay leaf to taste
  4. Take the freshly boiled water and add it to shallow dish
  5. Mix it all. Place the porkchops in and wait at least 30 minutes (to maximize deliciousness)

For the porkchops

  1. Place the skillet on the middle rack in your oven. Preheat your oven to 400 degrees Fahrenheit
  2. Remove the porkchops from the brine or their packaging. If removing from brine pat dry with a paper towel.
  3. Rub both sides of the porkchops with olive oil, then season them with salt and pepepr
  4. Use oven mitts to remove the skillet from the oven, then place it on the stove top at medium-high heat. It would be wise to turn on a vent fan or open a window
  5. Lay the porkchops on the hot skillet. They should start to sizzle immediately. Sear until the undersides of the chop are golden. This should take about 3 minutes. Don't panic if they start to smoke a little bit
  6. Flip the chops and immediately transfer the skillet to the oven. Let the chops roast until the thickest part of the meat registers an internal temperature of 140-145 degrees fahrenheit. This should take 6-10 minutes
  7. Rest the chops. Tent them loosely in foil for 5 minutes before serving
